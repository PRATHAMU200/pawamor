First Aid Essentials

  • Basic Pet First Aid Kit

    Must-have items: Gauze pads, adhesive tape, scissors, tweezers, digital thermometer, hydrogen peroxide 3%, saline solution, antimicrobial ointment, towels, muzzle (even friendly dogs may bite when in pain), emergency contact numbers

  • CPR for Pets

    Check for breathing and pulse. For dogs over 30 lbs: 100 compressions per minute. For smaller pets: 120 compressions per minute. Give rescue breaths every 30 compressions. Continue until help arrives.

  • Choking Response

    Check mouth for visible obstruction. Perform modified Heimlich: Place hands on both sides of ribcage, give sharp thrust inward and upward. For small pets, hold upside down and give firm back thumps between shoulder blades.

  • Bleeding Control

    Apply direct pressure with clean cloth. Maintain pressure for 3-5 minutes. If bleeding soaks through, add more material on top. For severe bleeding, apply pressure to pressure points while heading to emergency vet.

  • Poisoning Protocol

    Contact poison control IMMEDIATELY. Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed. Bring the substance container if possible. Common toxins: chocolate, grapes, xylitol, lilies (cats), antifreeze, rat poison, NSAIDs.

Health & Wellness Tips

  • Daily Health Checks

    Eyes: Clear, no discharge. Ears: Clean, no odor. Nose: Slightly moist. Teeth: No bad breath or tartar. Skin: No lumps, bumps, or irritation. Gait: Normal movement, no limping.

  • Dental Care

    Brush teeth 2-3 times weekly with pet-safe toothpaste. Provide dental chews. Schedule professional cleanings as recommended by vet. Dental disease affects 80% of pets over age 3.

  • Weight Management

    Feel ribs easily without pressing? Good. See a defined waist from above? Good. Visible tuck behind ribs? Good. If unsure, consult your vet. Obesity leads to diabetes, arthritis, heart disease.

  • Parasite Prevention

    Year-round flea/tick prevention. Monthly heartworm prevention. Regular deworming. Check for ticks after outdoor activities. Wash bedding weekly in hot water.

  • Vaccination Schedule

    Follow your vet's recommendations. Core vaccines for dogs: Rabies, Distemper, Parvovirus, Adenovirus. Core vaccines for cats: Rabies, Feline Distemper, Feline Herpesvirus, Calicivirus.

Nutrition Guidelines

  • Foods to NEVER Feed Pets

    Dogs/Cats: Chocolate, grapes/raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol, alcohol, caffeine, avocado, macadamia nuts, raw dough. Cats specifically: Dairy products (most cats are lactose intolerant).

  • Healthy Treat Options

    Dogs: Carrots, green beans, apple slices (no seeds), blueberries, plain cooked chicken, pumpkin. Cats: Cooked salmon, plain chicken, cat grass, small amounts of cooked egg.

  • Reading Pet Food Labels

    Look for named protein source as first ingredient. Avoid by-products and fillers. Check for AAFCO statement. Consider life stage appropriateness. Grain-free isn't automatically better.

  • Hydration Matters

    Fresh water always available. Clean bowls daily. Consider pet fountain to encourage drinking. Dogs need ~1 oz per pound of body weight daily. Cats often need encouragement to drink enough.

Training & Behavior

  • Positive Reinforcement Basics

    Reward desired behaviors immediately. Use high-value treats. Keep sessions short (5-10 minutes). End on a positive note. Consistency is key. Never use punishment-based methods.

  • Understanding Body Language

    Dogs: Relaxed = loose body, soft eyes, wagging tail. Stressed = yawning, lip licking, whale eye, tucked tail. Cats: Happy = slow blinks, upright tail, purring. Upset = flattened ears, dilated pupils, twitching tail.

  • Socialization Importance

    Critical period: 3-14 weeks for puppies, 2-7 weeks for kittens. Expose to various people, animals, environments. Keep experiences positive. Properly socialized pets are confident, friendly, adaptable.

  • Common Behavior Issues

    Barking: Address underlying cause. Chewing: Provide appropriate outlets. Scratching: Multiple scratching posts. Aggression: Consult professional trainer immediately. Anxiety: Create safe spaces, consider calming aids.

Pet-Proofing Your Home

  • Kitchen Safety

    Secure trash cans. Store food in pet-proof containers. Keep cleaning products locked away. Unplug small appliances. Cover stove knobs. Check for small objects that could be choking hazards.

  • Living Areas

    Secure electrical cords. Remove toxic plants. Anchor heavy furniture. Keep small objects out of reach. Use cord covers. Install baby gates for restricted areas.

  • Outdoor Spaces

    Secure fencing (check for gaps). Remove toxic plants. Store garden chemicals safely. Provide shade and water. Check for escape routes. Remove standing water to prevent mosquitoes.

  • Toxic Plants to Remove

    Dogs/Cats: Lilies, azaleas, oleander, sago palm, tulips, daffodils, chrysanthemums. Cats especially: All lily varieties (even pollen), poinsettias, philodendrons, pothos.
